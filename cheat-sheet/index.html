Biomes & Food Security

Geography
BiomeLarge region sharing similar climate, vegetation & wildlife.
Key BiomesTropical rainforest, grassland, desert, tundra, temperate forest.
Food SecurityReliable access to sufficient, nutritious food for all people.
4 PillarsAvailability · Access · Utilisation · Stability
⚠ Threats to Food Security Climate change, deforestation, overpopulation, water scarcity, soil degradation.
✅ Solutions Sustainable farming, crop rotation, irrigation technology, reducing food waste.

First Fleet (1788)

History
What11 ships from Britain, ~1,400 people including 759 convicts.
Who LedGovernor Arthur Phillip
LandedSydney Cove (Port Jackson), 26 Jan 1788
PurposeEstablish NSW as a British penal colony
ImpactLand dispossession, disease & cultural disruption for Aboriginal Australians

Economics — Key Terms

Economics
Demand
Consumer willingness & ability to buy at various prices.
Supply
Producer willingness & ability to sell at various prices.
Equilibrium
Price where quantity supplied equals quantity demanded.
Inflation
General rise in prices over time, reducing purchasing power.
GDP
Total value of all goods & services produced in a country.
Opportunity Cost
Value of the next best alternative that is given up.
Scarcity
Limited resources vs unlimited human wants — the core problem.
Mixed Economy
Free market combined with government intervention (e.g. Australia).
Fiscal Policy
Government use of spending & taxation to influence the economy.
